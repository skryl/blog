title: About me
---

I'm Alex Skryl — engineer, founder, and self-described maker of things.

I studied Computer Engineering and Chemistry at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. After graduating, I spent a few years doing R&D at Enova Financial before joining Trunk Club as their first software engineer. Watching a small team build something from nothing and eventually sell it to Nordstrom taught me more about building products than any class ever did.

I went on to co-found Trusted Child Care, which was later acquired by Care.com. In 2019, I started [NutriSense](https://www.nutrisense.io/), a digital health company that uses continuous glucose monitoring and machine learning to help people understand how their bodies respond to food. Heart disease runs in my family, and I wanted to build something that could help people — myself included — make better decisions about their health before it's too late.

On the technical side, my roots are in Ruby and Rails, but my interests have drifted toward lower-level and more exotic territory over the years: hardware description languages, compilers, and more recently, machine learning frameworks. Some of my open source work includes [mlx-ruby](https://github.com/skryl/mlx-ruby) (Ruby bindings for Apple's MLX framework), [rhdl](https://github.com/skryl/rhdl) (a hardware description language in Ruby), and [fuzzy_notes](https://github.com/skryl/fuzzy_notes) (a CLI note manager with fuzzy search).

Outside of work, I read a lot — somewhere north of 600 books on Goodreads at last count. I have a soft spot for mechanical keyboards, which I've written about before, and I run enough to justify building a REST client for the Nike+ API. I collect ideas across too many notebooks and occasionally manage to turn them into something useful.

This blog is called Skryl — it's my last name, and a place to put ideas down in writing.
